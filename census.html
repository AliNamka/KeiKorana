Khoi-San Census 2020

Race is still a sensitive issue all over the world.

In South Africa, a very diverse country with eleven official languages, it becomes more evident that for more than twenty years of democracy, not all of us are enjoying the freedom and equality enshrined in our constitution.

The Khoi and San communities of South Africa are still considered and classified as coloureds. What are coloureds? Where from do they originate? There are different references to different people as being coloured. Coloured people have no trace of original language or land where ever they found themselves are marginalized and vulnerable. The common understanding is that coloured people is the result of any Europe and non European union. Colonial legislation of 1928 make reference to the term of British Coloured people to be consided to receive social grants and pensions.These people excluded Africans and indigenous Khoi and San people.

The colonial 1950 racial classification act went further to classify different types of coloureds excluding Africans and indigenous Khoi and San communities.

Coloured people are the product of legislation. Khoi and San people are the product of their languages, heritage, culture and history.

People have the universal right of self-identification.

The coloured label was enforced on indigenous people by legislation policy and social systems like schools and religious institution.As an indigenous Khoi and San person you did not exist unless you except excepted being called coloured. It was a battle for more than twenty years for Khoi and San indigenous authorities known as the National Khoi and San Council to have our communities recognized by legislation an anticipation of implementation identify yourself.

You have the right to self-identification. Exercise your right by completing the Khoi and San National Census 2020 for Khoi and San people by Khoi and San people.

TOGETHER, WE WILL OVERCOME!
